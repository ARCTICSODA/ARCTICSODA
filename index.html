Abstract

With the emergence of text-to-image (T2I) generative models and innovative 3D representations such as 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), a significant increase in text-driven editing methods that leverage 2D priors generated by T2I models to optimize 3D scenes occurred. However, these methods still face significant challenges, such as imprecise localization and poor multiview consistency of 2D priors. Motivated by attention mechanism and diffusion model-based optimization, we introduce EpicSculptor, an innovative framework for 3DGS editing. On the basis of selecting target objects via Gaussian tracking, this approach leverages the cross-attention mechanism of T2I models to identify sub-objects with a higher degree of association with specific labels. By integrating the extended attributes of the corresponding Gaussian points to delimit the editable regions, the issue of imprecise localization is effectively addressed. This method achieves precise sub-object-level targeting for editing by marking Gaussian points with a higher degree of relevance to the objects. Furthermore, we propose an innovative multimodal and multiview joint optimization approach for 3DGS. This approach integrates the multimodal multiview score distillation sampling (MMV-SDS) loss, which estimates noise by leveraging text prompts, reference images, and multiview sampling images. Through delving into the interconnections among these elements, it steers the optimization process towards designated patterns, thereby ensuring that the optimization process efficaciously resolves the issue of inadequate multiview consistency of 2D priors. The empirical findings across four datasets underscore that our approach consistently achieves precise control over the editing areas and visually coherent output in both qualitative and quantitative assessments, achieving a 6.25\% increase in peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR).
